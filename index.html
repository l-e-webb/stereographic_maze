Stereographic Maze

Stereographic Maze is a simple math visualization / puzzle game. You are navigating a maze on the surface of a sphere, but the maze is being visualized on the plane via stereographic projection. Simply put, stereographic projection is a way of forming a 2D map from a sphere by projecting along lines from the north pole to a plane that passes through the equator. Under stereographic projection, the southern hemisphere appears in a circle in the middle of the plane, and the northern hemisphere is the entire remainder of the plane, stretching off infinitely in every direction, with the north pole itself being lost in the infinite distance.

In Stereographic Maze, our intrepid player (a dot) starts on the south pole, and must find its way to the north pole (also a dot). Use the arrow keys to move along the surface of the sphere to eventually reach the north pole; or, more accurately, use the arrow keys to rotate the entire sphere to get the original north pole to the center of the screen.
